The Problem With Welfare Today

Why a new approach is needed

Seafarers' welfare has relied on the same model for decades: independent port clubs, each working alone, invisible to the seafarer until they happen to find the door. The numbers show why that is no longer enough.

The Old Model

Isolated port clubs

  • Every seafarers' centre works alone, with its own systems
  • A seafarer has no way to know what's available before arriving in port
  • Information is outdated, paper-based or word-of-mouth
  • No shared data — no way to measure impact or improve
The IMWIRSA Model

One connected ecosystem

  • A single digital layer connects every port, centre and volunteer
  • Live, curated information — updated by a real local coordinator
  • One app, one experience, in the seafarer's own language
  • Evidence-based, measurable, continuously improved

From stepping off the gangway to finding real help — in four steps.

01

No installation — it opens straight in the browser, and remembers your language and preferences.

Why it matters

Long contracts and isolation take a measurable toll on mental health. A short, human moment of care — physical or psychological — measurably improves mood, focus and safety on the next watch.
