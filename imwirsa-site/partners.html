No Surprises

Conditions of joining the pilot

Not required

No hidden costs

  • No mandatory financial contribution
  • No restructuring your organisation
  • No change to your charter or governance
  • No exclusivity — keep working with other networks
  • No new software or hardware to buy
What's needed

Just one committed person

  • One Port Welfare Manager from your centre
  • 15–20 minutes a week to keep the port card current
  • Telegram on the coordinator's phone
  • A 30-minute introductory call, online
  • Willingness to collaborate — participation is fully voluntary, and you can leave at any time
The Person Behind Every Port

Port Welfare Manager

The project's ambassador in your port — a staff member or volunteer from your centre, officially accredited by IMWIRSA.

🧭

Main platform contact

The first person a seafarer reaches through MWApp for anything about your port.

🔄

Keeps data current

Updates the port card via the Telegram bot and responds to reports of outdated info.

🌐

Network participant

Joins international IMWIRSA network meetings and receives monthly analytics for their port.

Recognition options

IMWIRSA offers a flexible model — the organisation and PWM choose what fits.

🏅

A. Status & recognition

Official IMWIRSA certificate, a named page on the platform site, a reference letter, and access to the private PWM community.

💶

B. Financial support

Where budget allows: a monthly stipend (€50–150, based on port size and activity), connectivity costs covered, and an annual "Best PWM" award.

📈

C. Organisational growth

Free promotion of your centre, access to IMWIRSA grant programmes, staff training, and priority access to new features.
